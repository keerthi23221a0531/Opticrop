# Literature Survey & Social and Business Impact

## Literature Survey
A literature survey was conducted to study existing crop recommendation techniques and machine learning approaches used in precision agriculture, including classification-based recommendation systems, clustering-based pattern discovery, and regression-based yield prediction models. The survey helped identify commonly used environmental parameters (N, P, K, temperature, humidity, pH, rainfall) and algorithms (KNN, K-Means Clustering, Logistic Regression) suitable for crop suitability prediction.

## Social and Business Impact
An AI-driven crop recommendation solution has significant social and business value:

- **Social Impact:** Helps farmers make informed, data-driven decisions, reducing crop failure risk and improving food security. Supports sustainable farming practices by matching crops to suitable environmental conditions, thereby optimizing resource (water, fertilizer) usage.
- **Business Impact:** Enables agri-tech companies, cooperatives, and government agencies to offer value-added advisory services to farmers. Supports policymakers and researchers in identifying crop-environment patterns for regional agricultural planning.

## Business Requirements (Summary)
- Gather and analyze business requirements to understand project objectives and expected outcomes.
- The system should accept soil/environmental inputs and return a crop recommendation with high accuracy.
- The system should be usable via a simple web interface accessible to non-technical users (farmers).
